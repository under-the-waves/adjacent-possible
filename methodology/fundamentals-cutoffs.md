# Fundamentals Cutoffs

Cutoffs the [prioritisation survey]({{ site.baseurl }}/prioritisation-survey/) uses to flag a "significant gap" in five upstream areas. Each targets where the harm curve steepens. Validated short forms are used where they exist (PHQ-2, GAD-2, WHO-5, UCLA-3, AUDIT-C). The screen errs toward false positives: a wrongly-surfaced flag costs an override click; a missed one defeats the purpose.

| Area | Cutoff | Screen | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sleep** | Under 6 hours per night on typical work nights | Sleep-duration bands; flag the bottom two | Cappuccio et al. (2010), *Sleep* meta-analysis (1.4M participants): mortality rises 12% below 6 hours, concentrated below 6 |
| **Mental health** (sub-clinical) | WHO-5 ≤ 13 (≤52%) | Five WHO-5 items, summed | [Topp et al. (2015)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25831962/) systematic review; Bech et al. (2003) for the 50% cutoff |
| **Foundational finances** | Either liquid savings under $1,000 or revolving balances above 15% APR | Two yes/no items: $400 unexpected expense; high-interest debt | [Mani et al. (2013)](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1238041) on scarcity load (~13 IQ points); [Federal Reserve SHED](https://www.federalreserve.gov/publications/report-economic-well-being-us-households.htm) on the $400 question |
| **Connection** | Either UCLA-3 score ≥ 6 or less than weekly meaningful contact with close friends or family | UCLA-3 plus contact-frequency item | [Holt-Lunstad et al. (2015)](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1745691614568352) meta-analysis (3.4M participants): isolation mortality risk comparable to smoking 15 a day |
| **Activity and nutrition** | Either under 60 min/week moderate-to-vigorous activity, or fewer than 3 daily servings of vegetables or fruit | Activity-minutes bands; fruit-and-veg servings | Lee et al. (2012), *Lancet* on physical inactivity; Aune et al. (2017) on the fruit-and-veg dose-response |

Notes:

- Triage catches clinical depression (PHQ-2 ≥ 3) and anxiety (GAD-2 ≥ 3); WHO-5 covers sub-clinical wellbeing impairment that clears those screens.
- Mortgage and student debt are excluded; their harm curves are far flatter than revolving consumer credit. Acute distress is handled in triage.
- Subjective loneliness and contact frequency are partly independent, so flagging on either keeps false negatives low.
- The activity cutoff targets the inactive end – the gain from 0 to 60 minutes a week is much larger than from 150 to 300.
- Broader dietary issues (ultra-processed share, sugar, fibre) live in the [Nutrition life area]({{ site.baseurl }}/nutrition/).

These cutoffs are stated with more precision than the evidence supports. They will move as the screen accumulates response data.
