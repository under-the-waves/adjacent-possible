# Global Impact

## Why Global Impact Matters

Charitable giving and effective philanthropy represent one of the most powerful ways individuals can improve the world beyond their immediate circle. Americans gave $557 billion to charity in 2023, approximately 2% of GDP, yet the effectiveness of these donations varies enormously <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-giving-scale')">i</span>. Research on charity effectiveness reveals that the best interventions can be over 100 times more cost-effective than average ones -- meaning that where you give matters far more than how much <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-effectiveness')">i</span>.

Evidence-based giving has demonstrated remarkable results. GiveWell, one of the leading charity evaluators, has directed over $2.6 billion to its recommended charities, contributing to an estimated 340,000 lives saved and significant improvements in wellbeing across low-income countries <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-givewell')">i</span>. Beyond direct giving, pledging movements like Founders Pledge (over $10 billion pledged by entrepreneurs) and Giving What We Can show that systematic commitment to effective philanthropy can compound over a lifetime. Whether through direct donations, career choices, or advocacy, deliberate attention to global impact is one of the highest-leverage uses of personal resources.

## Global Impact Values

Your approach to global impact depends on what aspects you value most. This guide balances three core values, with percentages indicating the relative weight given to each in our recommendations.

### Effectiveness (40%)
Maximising the actual impact per unit of resource deployed -- ensuring that your time, money, and effort produce the greatest possible improvement in lives. This includes researching charity effectiveness, choosing evidence-backed interventions, and measuring outcomes rather than relying on intuition or emotional appeal. People who prioritise this value treat philanthropy as a problem to be optimised.

### Commitment (35%)
Sustained, systematic dedication to global impact over time -- moving beyond occasional donations to a deliberate, planned approach to giving. This includes setting giving targets, making pledges, integrating impact into career decisions, and building habits that compound over years. Those who prioritise this value recognise that consistency matters as much as any single decision.

### Breadth (25%)
Engaging with global impact through multiple channels beyond direct donations -- including advocacy, career choice, consumption decisions, volunteering skills, and influencing others to give effectively. This includes understanding systemic levers for change and contributing through whatever means best match your capabilities. People who prioritise this value seek to maximise their total footprint of positive impact.

## Benchmarks by Level

Despite widespread charitable intent, most giving is neither strategic nor evidence-based. The average American donates around 2% of income, but few evaluate the effectiveness of their chosen charities. Only a small fraction of donors use charity evaluators, and emotional appeal drives more giving decisions than evidence of impact. Deliberate, effectiveness-focused philanthropy is genuinely rare, and sustained commitment to optimised giving places individuals well above population norms.

### Level 1: Awareness

**Effectiveness**: Understand the concept of cost-effectiveness in charity -- that different interventions produce vastly different outcomes per pound spent <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-effectiveness')">i</span>

**Commitment**: Know your current giving patterns -- how much you donate annually, to whom, and why <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-commitment')">i</span>

**Breadth**: Identify the channels beyond direct donation through which you currently contribute to or could contribute to global welfare <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-breadth')">i</span>

### Level 2: Foundation (80th percentile capability)

**Effectiveness**: Donations directed primarily to evidence-backed charities evaluated by organisations like GiveWell, The Life You Can Save, or Animal Charity Evaluators <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-effectiveness')">i</span>

**Commitment**: A consistent giving practice of at least 1 -- 3% of income annually, with a clear budget and regular donations rather than sporadic impulse giving <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-commitment')">i</span>

**Breadth**: Active engagement with at least one non-donation channel -- ethical consumption, workplace advocacy, or volunteering skills to effective organisations <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-breadth')">i</span>

### Level 3: Proficiency (95th percentile capability)

**Effectiveness**: Deep understanding of cause prioritisation, with giving allocated across a researched portfolio of high-impact interventions and regular review of evidence <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-effectiveness')">i</span>

**Commitment**: Giving at 5 -- 10% of income with a formal pledge or commitment mechanism, integrated into financial planning as a non-negotiable line item <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-commitment')">i</span>

**Breadth**: Multiple impact channels active -- effective giving, career capital building, advocacy, and influencing others toward evidence-based philanthropy <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-breadth')">i</span>

### Level 4: Excellence (99th percentile capability)

**Effectiveness**: Sophisticated understanding of global priorities, counterfactual reasoning, and impact measurement, with giving strategy informed by the latest research and adjusted accordingly <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-effectiveness')">i</span>

**Commitment**: Giving at 10%+ of income or equivalent career impact, with a lifetime giving plan and significant resources directed toward the most pressing global problems <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-commitment')">i</span>

**Breadth**: A comprehensive impact portfolio spanning donations, career choice, advocacy, mentoring other givers, and systemic change efforts -- each channel deliberately optimised <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-breadth')">i</span>

### Level 5: Mastery (99.9th percentile capability)

**Effectiveness**: Among the most informed and strategic philanthropists at your resource level, with a giving approach that rivals professional grant-makers in rigour and impact per pound <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-effectiveness')">i</span>

**Commitment**: Extraordinary sustained commitment -- whether through major pledges, career dedication to high-impact work, or lifetime giving that represents a transformative share of resources <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-commitment')">i</span>

**Breadth**: A life organised around maximising positive global impact across every available channel, with influence that extends far beyond personal giving through community building, advocacy, and systemic change <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-breadth')">i</span>
