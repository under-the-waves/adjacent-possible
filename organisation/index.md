# Organisation

## Why Organisation Matters

Personal organisation is the infrastructure that determines how effectively you convert intention into action. Despite its fundamental importance, 82% of people have no formal time management system <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-no-system')">i</span>, and the average person spends 8.5 minutes per day searching for misplaced items <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-searching-items')">i</span>. In the workplace, the costs are even steeper: knowledge workers spend between 1.8 and 2.5 hours per day searching for information they need to do their jobs <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-information-search')">i</span>.

The downstream effects of disorganisation extend well beyond lost time. 41% of to-do list items are never completed <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-incomplete-tasks')">i</span>, 52% of people missed a healthcare appointment in the past year (with a third of those simply because they forgot) <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-missed-appointments')">i</span>, and 54% of people report feeling overwhelmed by clutter -- with 80% of household clutter stemming from disorganisation rather than lack of space <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-clutter')">i</span>. Organisation is not about tidiness for its own sake; it is the scaffolding that supports reliability, clarity, and the ability to direct your energy towards what actually matters.

## Organisation Values

Your approach to organisation depends on what aspects you value most. This guide balances three core values, with percentages indicating the relative weight given to each in our recommendations.

For personalised recommendations based on your unique priorities, visit [Organisation Personalised]({{ site.baseurl }}/organisation/personalised), where you can adjust these value weightings to see which interventions work best for your specific goals and preferences.

### Speed (35%)
Minimising the time spent on organisational overhead so you can move quickly from intention to action. This includes rapid processing of incoming tasks, fast retrieval of information, quick decision-making about priorities, and systems designed for throughput over perfection. People who prioritise this value accept occasional errors or missed details in exchange for getting more done in less time.

### Thoroughness (35%)
Ensuring nothing important falls through the cracks through comprehensive tracking, careful review, and meticulous follow-through. This includes capturing every commitment, checking work before marking it complete, maintaining detailed records, and building systems that catch errors and omissions. People who prioritise this value accept slower processing in exchange for higher reliability and fewer missed obligations.

### Simplicity (30%)
Keeping organisational systems as minimal and intuitive as possible, with the fewest tools, steps, and rules needed. This includes resisting feature creep, choosing robust over clever solutions, and preferring a single trusted system over a complex ecosystem. People who prioritise this value build systems they can actually maintain long-term, even at the cost of some capability or rigour.

## Benchmarks by Level

Research paints a stark picture of how most people manage -- or fail to manage -- their personal systems. The average person <a href="https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/lost-and-found-the-average-american-spends-25-days-each-year-looking-for-lost-items-collectively-costing-us-households-27-billion-annually-in-replacement-costs-300449305.html" target="_blank">loses over an hour per week to searching for misplaced items</a> alone, and knowledge workers spend <a href="https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/the-social-economy" target="_blank">roughly a quarter of their day hunting for information</a> rather than using it. <a href="https://blog.idonethis.com/how-to-master-the-art-of-to-do-lists/" target="_blank">Nearly half of all intended tasks go uncompleted</a>, and <a href="https://www.unitedhealthgroup.com/newsroom/2022/2022-07-20-optum-consumer-survey-better-tools-for-finding-care.html" target="_blank">over half the population misses medical appointments</a> due to poor tracking. These baselines mean that even basic organisational competence places you well above the median, and genuine mastery is vanishingly rare.

### Level 1: Awareness

**Speed**: Spend 8 - 10 minutes per day searching for misplaced items; lose 2+ hours per day at work searching for information; no consistent storage or retrieval system <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-speed')">i</span>

**Thoroughness**: 41% of intended tasks never completed; 52% chance of missing a healthcare appointment in the past year; no trusted capture system for commitments <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-thoroughness')">i</span>

**Simplicity**: Systems are either non-existent or a chaotic patchwork of apps, notes, and memory; no coherent organisational approach <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-simplicity')">i</span>

### Level 2: Foundation (80th percentile capability)

**Speed**: Spend fewer than 3 minutes per day searching for items; retrieve any document within 5 minutes; process incoming tasks within 24 hours <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-speed')">i</span>

**Thoroughness**: Fewer than 3 missed commitments per month; 75%+ task completion rate; say-do ratio of 80% or higher <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-thoroughness')">i</span>

**Simplicity**: Use 1 - 2 core tools consistently rather than a scattered collection; maintain a single capture point for all incoming tasks and ideas <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-simplicity')">i</span>

### Level 3: Proficiency (95th percentile capability)

**Speed**: Rarely search for anything; retrieve any document within 60 seconds; achieve daily inbox-zero across all capture points <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-speed')">i</span>

**Thoroughness**: Fewer than 2 missed commitments per quarter; 90%+ task completion rate; say-do ratio of 90% or higher <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-thoroughness')">i</span>

**Simplicity**: Organisational system fits on one page; new tools or processes added only when existing ones prove inadequate; minimal maintenance overhead <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-simplicity')">i</span>

### Level 4: Excellence (99th percentile capability)

**Speed**: Retrieve any item in seconds; digital retrieval under 30 seconds; routine decisions systematised and largely automated <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-speed')">i</span>

**Thoroughness**: Approximately zero missed commitments per year; say-do ratio of 97% or higher; proactive risk flagging for anything at risk of slipping <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-thoroughness')">i</span>

**Simplicity**: System is so streamlined that someone else could learn it in an hour; every component earns its place through demonstrated value; elegantly handles complexity without becoming complex itself <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-simplicity')">i</span>

### Level 5: Mastery (99.9th percentile capability)

**Speed**: Zero search time; fully integrated physical and digital systems with seamless transitions; zero cognitive overhead from organisational maintenance <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-speed')">i</span>

**Thoroughness**: Say-do ratio of 99% or higher; systems resilient to disruption, illness, travel, and life changes; thoroughness maintained without conscious effort <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-thoroughness')">i</span>

**Simplicity**: "Mind like water" -- complete trust in a system so minimal and intuitive that it feels like an extension of thought rather than a separate practice; system survives life changes without needing redesign <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-simplicity')">i</span>
