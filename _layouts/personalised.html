How scoring works

Each intervention is scored on a logarithmic scale per value, where each point represents roughly 2× the impact. Per-value scores combine the magnitude of benefit, the success rate, and adherence; positive values mean the intervention helps, negative values mean it harms. The Weighted Benefit Score (WBS) sums these per-value scores using your slider weights to rank interventions for your priorities.

The Driver column shows which value is contributing most to each intervention’s ranking for you. If you weight wealth highly but an intervention’s biggest effect for you is on wellbeing, that’s reflected here. Time and Money EROI show efficiency – higher means more benefit per hour or dollar invested.

When you complete the {{ page.domain_label | default: page.domain | capitalize }} Awareness assessment while signed in, rankings shrink for values where you’re already well above the population baseline – there’s less room to improve, so interventions targeting those values score lower for you.
