# Romantic Relationships

## Why Romantic Relationships Matter

Romantic partnerships are among the strongest predictors of life satisfaction and health outcomes. Well over 90% of people marry at some point in their lives, yet 40 -- 50% of marriages end in divorce <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-divorce-rates')">i</span>. For most couples, marital satisfaction does not decline over time but remains relatively stable -- challenging the conventional narrative of inevitable deterioration. However, by the time the first child reaches 15, parents' marital satisfaction has declined on average by almost one standard deviation <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-satisfaction-trends')">i</span>.

The predictors of relationship quality are well-studied. Satisfaction correlates at 0.34 with communication quality, 0.36 with togetherness, and -0.35 with frequency of disagreements. Secure attachment predicts increasing satisfaction over time, while insecure attachment predicts lower and declining satisfaction <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-predictors')">i</span>. These findings suggest that relationship quality is not fixed by partner selection alone but is substantially shaped by the skills and habits both partners bring to the relationship.

## Romantic Relationships Values

Your approach to romantic relationships depends on what aspects you value most. This guide balances three core values, with percentages indicating the relative weight given to each in our recommendations.

### Intimacy (40%)
Emotional closeness, vulnerability, trust, and deep mutual understanding. This includes the ability to share your inner life with your partner, to feel genuinely known and accepted, and to maintain emotional connection through life's changes. People who prioritise this value invest in communication skills, emotional availability, and the courage to be vulnerable.

### Partnership (35%)
Effective collaboration on the practical dimensions of shared life -- finances, household management, parenting, decision-making, and navigating conflicts constructively. Those who prioritise this value focus on equitable division of responsibilities, aligned goals, and the ability to function as a reliable team.

### Growth (25%)
Mutual development and evolution within the relationship -- supporting each other's individual growth while deepening the relationship itself. This includes challenging each other constructively, adapting to changing needs, and maintaining novelty and vitality over time. People who prioritise this value seek relationships that make both partners better.

## Benchmarks by Level

Research reveals a paradox: most married people report being satisfied with their relationships, yet divorce rates remain high and many partnerships stagnate in comfortable mediocrity. Only about 30% of marriages maintain what researchers call "vital" or "total" quality over the long term. Communication quality, attachment security, and deliberate investment in the relationship are the strongest modifiable predictors of sustained satisfaction. Achieving consistently high relationship quality requires skills that most people never formally develop.

### Level 1: Awareness

**Intimacy**: Understand your own attachment style and its impact on how you connect, communicate, and handle conflict in relationships <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-intimacy')">i</span>

**Partnership**: Know your relationship's current strengths and pain points -- where you collaborate well and where friction consistently arises <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-partnership')">i</span>

**Growth**: Recognise whether your relationship is growing, stagnating, or declining, and identify what each partner needs to feel fulfilled <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-growth')">i</span>

### Level 2: Foundation (80th percentile capability)

**Intimacy**: Regular emotional check-ins with your partner, ability to express needs and feelings without escalation, and weekly quality time dedicated to connection <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-intimacy')">i</span>

**Partnership**: Clear division of household and financial responsibilities, constructive conflict resolution without contempt or stonewalling, and aligned approach to major decisions <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-partnership')">i</span>

**Growth**: Actively support each other's personal goals, introduce novelty into the relationship at least monthly, and discuss the relationship's direction openly <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-growth')">i</span>

### Level 3: Proficiency (95th percentile capability)

**Intimacy**: Deep emotional attunement where both partners feel genuinely understood, regular vulnerable conversations about fears and aspirations, and physical and emotional intimacy that both rate as highly satisfying <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-intimacy')">i</span>

**Partnership**: Seamless collaboration on logistics, finances, and parenting with minimal friction, effective repair after conflicts, and shared decision-making that consistently produces outcomes both partners support <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-partnership')">i</span>

**Growth**: Both partners demonstrably growing as individuals and as a couple, with the relationship serving as a catalyst for personal development and expanded capabilities <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-growth')">i</span>

### Level 4: Excellence (99th percentile capability)

**Intimacy**: A relationship characterised by profound trust, complete emotional safety, and the kind of deep knowing that comes from years of attentive, responsive connection <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-intimacy')">i</span>

**Partnership**: Exceptional teamwork maintained through major life transitions -- career changes, illness, parenting challenges, financial stress -- without sustained relationship deterioration <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-partnership')">i</span>

**Growth**: The relationship consistently generates new shared meaning, with both partners evolving and the relationship deepening in ways that surprise and delight both <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-growth')">i</span>

### Level 5: Mastery (99.9th percentile capability)

**Intimacy**: Sustained emotional and physical intimacy across decades that deepens rather than plateaus, with both partners reporting the relationship as a primary source of meaning and joy <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-intimacy')">i</span>

**Partnership**: A partnership that others look to as a model -- consistently effective collaboration, graceful conflict resolution, and the ability to navigate any challenge as a unified team <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-partnership')">i</span>

**Growth**: A relationship that has weathered significant adversity and emerged stronger each time, with both partners fundamentally transformed for the better by the relationship itself <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-growth')">i</span>
