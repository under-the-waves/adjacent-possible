# Goals

## Why Goals Matter

Goal-setting is one of the most studied and validated performance interventions in behavioural science. Specific, challenging goals consistently improve performance by 10 – 25% compared to vague intentions or no goals at all <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-locke-latham')">i</span>. Yet despite their power, the vast majority of people fail to use them effectively. Roughly 70% of US adults set goals of some kind, but only 33% define them with any specificity, and only 35% write them down <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-prevalence')">i</span>.

The gap between intention and achievement is stark: <a href="https://news.scranton.edu/articles/2021/01/fac-norcross-stories.shtml" target="_blank">92% of goal-setters never reach their goals</a>, and 80% of New Year's resolutions are abandoned by February. However, writing goals down increases achievement by 42%, and weekly progress monitoring significantly promotes attainment <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-effectiveness')">i</span>. The evidence is clear that the problem is not the concept of goals but the systems people use to pursue them.

## Goals Values

Your approach to goals depends on what aspects you value most. This guide balances three core values, with percentages indicating the relative weight given to each in our recommendations.

For personalised recommendations based on your unique priorities, visit [Goals Personalised]({{ site.baseurl }}/goals/personalised), where you can adjust these value weightings to see which interventions work best for your specific goals and preferences.

### Ambition (35%)
Setting goals that push beyond what feels comfortable and aiming for transformative outcomes rather than incremental progress. This includes stretch targets, multi-year visions, willingness to risk failure for high-impact results, and a default toward bigger rather than smaller goals. People who prioritise this value believe that aiming high -- even if you miss -- produces better outcomes than setting safe targets you know you can hit.

### Realism (35%)
Setting goals grounded in honest assessment of current capacity, available resources, and probability of success. This includes accounting for competing demands, building in buffers for unexpected setbacks, setting milestones that are genuinely achievable, and preferring steady reliable progress over ambitious targets that collapse under real-world constraints. People who prioritise this value believe that achievable goals sustained over years produce better outcomes than dramatic aspirations that lead to burnout or abandonment.

### Flexibility (30%)
Maintaining the ability to change course, adjust timelines, reprioritise, or abandon goals as new information emerges. This includes treating goals as hypotheses rather than commitments, running regular reviews to evaluate whether goals remain worth pursuing, and developing the skill of strategic retreat without emotional cost. People who prioritise this value believe that adapting intelligently is more important than persisting stubbornly.

## Benchmarks by Level

Research reveals how rare effective goal-setting actually is. Although roughly 70% of adults set goals, <a href="https://scholar.dominican.edu/psychology-faculty-conference-presentations/3/" target="_blank">only a third do so with any specificity</a>. Writing goals down — a simple act that <a href="https://scholar.dominican.edu/psychology-faculty-conference-presentations/3/" target="_blank">increases achievement by 42%</a> — is practised by just 35% of goal-setters. The <a href="https://news.scranton.edu/articles/2021/01/fac-norcross-stories.shtml" target="_blank">92% failure rate</a> means that anyone who consistently achieves their goals across multiple domains is already well into the upper percentiles of the population. Even modest improvements in goal clarity, tracking, and review place a person ahead of the vast majority.

### Level 1: Awareness

**Ambition**: Goals are modest and vague -- "get a bit healthier," "save some money" -- with no stretch component or transformative vision <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-ambition')">i</span>

**Realism**: No honest assessment of capacity or constraints; goals are set based on aspiration alone with no accounting for competing demands or likely obstacles <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-realism')">i</span>

**Flexibility**: Failed goals are simply abandoned with no reflection; no deliberate process for modifying, reprioritising, or strategically retiring goals <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-flexibility')">i</span>

### Level 2: Foundation (80th percentile capability)

**Ambition**: 3 – 5 written goals with at least one genuine stretch target; willing to set goals with a meaningful risk of failure <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-ambition')">i</span>

**Realism**: Goals account for existing commitments and available time; deadlines reflect honest capacity assessment rather than wishful thinking <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-realism')">i</span>

**Flexibility**: Can modify timelines or scope without abandoning goals entirely; recognises when a goal needs adjustment rather than more willpower <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-flexibility')">i</span>

### Level 3: Proficiency (95th percentile capability)

**Ambition**: Goal hierarchy (vision > annual > quarterly > weekly) with stretch targets at every level; actively pursues goals that would be transformative if achieved <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-ambition')">i</span>

**Realism**: Goals include built-in buffers for setbacks; milestones are calibrated against past performance data; competing priorities are explicitly triaged <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-realism')">i</span>

**Flexibility**: Deliberate goal triage based on data; able to deprioritise or retire goals using explicit criteria rather than guilt or avoidance <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-flexibility')">i</span>

### Level 4: Excellence (99th percentile capability)

**Ambition**: Multi-year transformative goals with pre-committed decision rules; actively balances a portfolio of ambitious bets with different risk profiles <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-ambition')">i</span>

**Realism**: Detailed resource and capacity modelling before committing to goals; historical completion data informs new goal calibration; proactive identification of constraints and bottlenecks <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-realism')">i</span>

**Flexibility**: Pre-mortems conducted for significant goals; systematic review cadence with explicit criteria for continuing, modifying, or retiring goals; strategic retreat practised without emotional cost <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-flexibility')">i</span>

### Level 5: Mastery (99.9th percentile capability)

**Ambition**: Multi-decade transformative vision with causal models linking daily actions to long-term outcomes; cross-domain coherence where ambitious goals in one area reinforce progress in others <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-ambition')">i</span>

**Realism**: 90%+ of goals achieved over a 10+ year track record; goal calibration so well-tuned that stretch targets and realistic assessment converge -- ambitious goals that reliably get done <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-realism')">i</span>

**Flexibility**: Proactive environmental scanning for emerging opportunities and threats; maintains a goal graveyard with documented rationale; goal-setting methodology itself evolves based on systematic review of what works <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-flexibility')">i</span>
