# Food Management

## Why Food Management Matters

Getting food on the table is one of the most repetitive and cognitively demanding tasks in daily life. Deciding what to eat is now rated as more burdensome than the actual cooking or cleanup, with 68% of Americans saying it is their biggest mealtime challenge. 77% report being too exhausted to cook after work <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-decision-fatigue')">i</span>. Yet the skills involved in planning, sourcing, and preparing food reliably are rarely taught systematically.

Food management is distinct from nutrition (which concerns *what* you eat) – it concerns *how you get it*. A well-run food system reduces daily stress, minimises waste, and ensures your nutritional goals are actually achievable in practice <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-food-literacy')">i</span>. Whether you cook from scratch, rely on meal kits, or outsource to restaurants and delivery services, the underlying competency is the same: can you reliably provision food that meets your needs?
