# Sex

## Why Sex Matters

Sexual health and satisfaction are important components of overall wellbeing that are often neglected in personal development frameworks. High importance of sexual health to quality of life is reported by 62% of men and 43% of women <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-importance')">i</span>. Sexual satisfaction is associated with greater mental and physical health, including improved cardiovascular health, decreased depression, lower mortality rates, and increased relationship satisfaction <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-health-benefits')">i</span>.

Yet sexual satisfaction is far from universal. Among those who are sexually active, satisfaction differs significantly by age, health status, and relationship quality. Lower loneliness, better health, and partnership satisfaction are the strongest predictors of sexual satisfaction <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-predictors')">i</span>. Synchronicity in sexual desire and open sexual communication are consistently associated with both higher frequency and higher satisfaction, suggesting that the quality of sexual connection depends heavily on communication skills and mutual attunement.

## Sex Values

Your approach to sexual wellbeing depends on what aspects you value most. This guide balances three core values, with percentages indicating the relative weight given to each in our recommendations.

### Connection (40%)
Sexual expression as a means of deepening emotional and physical intimacy with a partner. This includes mutual attunement, responsiveness to each other's needs and desires, open communication about preferences, and using physical intimacy to strengthen the relational bond. People who prioritise this value see sex primarily as a shared experience that builds closeness.

### Pleasure (35%)
The direct experience of physical and psychological enjoyment from sexual activity. This includes knowledge of your own body, ability to communicate desires, comfort with exploration, and the pursuit of mutually satisfying experiences. Those who prioritise this value focus on expanding their capacity for enjoyment and their partner's.

### Health (25%)
Sexual activity as a contributor to physical and mental health outcomes. This includes maintaining sexual function, addressing sexual health concerns proactively, understanding the connection between overall health and sexual wellbeing, and managing reproductive health effectively. People who prioritise this value approach sexuality through a wellness lens.

## Benchmarks by Level

Research reveals significant variation in sexual satisfaction across the population. Among sexually active adults, 55% of men and 45% of women reported sexual activity in the previous 30 days, but satisfaction levels varied substantially. Sexual frequency is a strong predictor of self-reported happiness, but the relationship between frequency and satisfaction plateaus -- quality and connection matter more than frequency beyond a baseline threshold. Open communication about sexual needs remains uncommon, creating a substantial gap between desired and actual sexual experiences for many people.

### Level 1: Awareness

**Connection**: Understand how sexual intimacy currently functions in your relationship and can identify what enhances or diminishes emotional closeness during sex <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-connection')">i</span>

**Pleasure**: Know your own body, preferences, and responses well enough to communicate basic desires and boundaries to a partner <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-pleasure')">i</span>

**Health**: Aware of your current sexual health status, practise appropriate protection, and know when to seek medical advice for sexual health concerns <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-health')">i</span>

### Level 2: Foundation (80th percentile capability)

**Connection**: Regular sexual intimacy that both partners describe as emotionally connecting, with open communication about needs and desires <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-connection')">i</span>

**Pleasure**: Both partners regularly experience satisfying sexual encounters, with the ability to communicate preferences and adjust to each other's responses <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-pleasure')">i</span>

**Health**: Up-to-date sexual health screening, proactive management of any sexual health concerns, and understanding of how lifestyle factors affect sexual function <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-health')">i</span>

### Level 3: Proficiency (95th percentile capability)

**Connection**: Sexual intimacy consistently deepens emotional connection, with both partners feeling fully present, attuned, and emotionally safe during sexual experiences <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-connection')">i</span>

**Pleasure**: High mutual satisfaction sustained over time, with comfort exploring new experiences and the ability to maintain novelty within a committed relationship <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-pleasure')">i</span>

**Health**: Optimal sexual function supported by overall health practices, with any age-related or health-related changes addressed proactively and adaptively <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-health')">i</span>

### Level 4: Excellence (99th percentile capability)

**Connection**: Sexual experiences that both partners describe as profoundly connecting, with a level of vulnerability, trust, and attunement that transcends physical pleasure <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-connection')">i</span>

**Pleasure**: Consistently exceptional mutual satisfaction maintained across years or decades, with both partners fully comfortable communicating evolving needs and exploring together <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-pleasure')">i</span>

**Health**: Sexual health optimised across all dimensions -- physical, psychological, and relational -- with the ability to adapt sexual expression to any health changes without loss of satisfaction <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-health')">i</span>

### Level 5: Mastery (99.9th percentile capability)

**Connection**: Sexual intimacy that serves as a primary source of emotional renewal and deepening connection, sustained across decades with continued growth in vulnerability and attunement <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-connection')">i</span>

**Pleasure**: Sustained, evolving sexual satisfaction across the lifespan that both partners rate as deeply fulfilling, with the flexibility and communication skills to adapt to any changes in desire, function, or circumstance <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-pleasure')">i</span>

**Health**: Comprehensive sexual health maintained across the lifespan, with proactive adaptation to aging and health changes, and the knowledge and willingness to seek support when needed <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-health')">i</span>
