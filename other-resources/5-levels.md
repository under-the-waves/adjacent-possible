# The Five Bands

Each life area is broken into five bands, anchored to population percentiles. They're reference points for where you sit, not a ladder to climb. Your values determine which areas are worth pushing further in.

## The Bands

### Awareness
You understand the life area and can assess your own position. No habit changes or financial investment required.

### Top 20%
Solid basics in place – more capable than 80% of the population on this value.

### Top 5%
Notably more capable than most people.

### Top 1%
Exceptional capability. Takes significant time and effort.

### Top 0.1%
Roughly 1 in 1,000 people. Substantial personal mastery, though not "the best in the world".

## Why Percentiles

Percentile is the most neutral anchor – rather than the framework deciding what "good" looks like, the population does. The behavioural benchmarks shown for each band are best-guesses at what that band looks like in practice. They help you self-locate, but the underlying definition is the percentile.

Population distributions are not the same shape across life areas. For some – like fitness – moving up a band takes years. For others – like having a clear sense of purpose – a single decision can move you several bands at once because most people are bunched at the bottom. The bands tell you *where you are*, not how hard it was to get there.
