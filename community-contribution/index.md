# Community Contribution

## Why Community Contribution Matters

Contributing to your local community -- through volunteering, civic participation, and neighbourhood involvement -- has measurable benefits for both communities and individuals. In 2021, 23.2% of American adults formally volunteered, contributing 4.1 billion hours of service valued at $122.9 billion <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-volunteering-scale')">i</span>. Yet the benefits flow both ways: volunteers consistently report better mental health, with research showing lower rates of depression and anxiety, higher life satisfaction, and greater sense of purpose compared to non-volunteers <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-volunteer-wellbeing')">i</span>.

The health benefits of community contribution are dose-dependent and substantial. Volunteering approximately 100 hours per year (about 2 hours per week) appears to be the threshold for significant health benefits. Those who volunteer 200 or more hours annually show a 40% reduction in hypertension risk. Social integration through community involvement also reduces mortality risk and improves cognitive function in older adults <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-health-benefits')">i</span>. Beyond individual benefits, community contribution builds social capital -- the networks of trust and reciprocity that make communities function. In an era of declining civic participation and increasing social isolation, deliberate community contribution is both increasingly valuable and increasingly rare.

## Community Contribution Values

Your approach to community contribution depends on what aspects you value most. This guide balances three core values, with percentages indicating the relative weight given to each in our recommendations.

### Service (40%)
Direct contribution of time, skills, and effort to community needs -- volunteering, mentoring, organising, and hands-on participation in local improvement. This includes regular commitment to organisations, informal help for neighbours, and willingness to contribute labour where it is needed most. People who prioritise this value show up consistently and do the work.

### Civic Engagement (35%)
Participation in the democratic and institutional life of your community -- voting, attending local meetings, engaging with local government, supporting community organisations, and staying informed about local issues. This includes understanding how local decisions affect your community and making your voice heard in those processes. Those who prioritise this value strengthen the systems that serve everyone.

### Social Capital (25%)
Building the networks of trust, reciprocity, and connection that make communities resilient. This includes knowing your neighbours, participating in local events, bridging divides between different groups, and fostering the social infrastructure that enables collective action. People who prioritise this value invest in the relationships that hold communities together.

## Benchmarks by Level

Despite the documented benefits of community contribution, participation rates have declined significantly over recent decades. Only 23% of adults formally volunteer, down from historical highs. Civic engagement beyond voting is even rarer -- fewer than 10% of residents attend local government meetings, and neighbourhood social ties have weakened considerably. Against this backdrop, even modest, regular community contribution represents above-average civic behaviour. Sustained, multi-channel community engagement is genuinely exceptional.

### Level 1: Awareness

**Service**: Know which community organisations operate in your area and what volunteer opportunities are available <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-service')">i</span>

**Civic Engagement**: Understand the basic structure of your local government, know your elected representatives, and vote consistently in local elections <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-civic')">i</span>

**Social Capital**: Know your immediate neighbours by name and have a basic sense of the social dynamics in your local community <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-social')">i</span>

### Level 2: Foundation (80th percentile capability)

**Service**: Regular volunteering commitment of at least 2 -- 4 hours per month with a local organisation, contributing meaningfully to community needs <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-service')">i</span>

**Civic Engagement**: Informed participation in local elections, awareness of key local issues, and occasional engagement with community decision-making processes <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-civic')">i</span>

**Social Capital**: Active relationships with neighbours and participation in at least one community group, event series, or local organisation <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-social')">i</span>

### Level 3: Proficiency (95th percentile capability)

**Service**: Consistent volunteering of 100+ hours per year, with skills-based contributions that leverage your professional expertise for community benefit <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-service')">i</span>

**Civic Engagement**: Regular attendance at local meetings, engagement with planning and policy decisions, and active relationships with local representatives <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-civic')">i</span>

**Social Capital**: A broad local network spanning different community groups, with a reputation as someone who connects people and strengthens neighbourhood ties <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-social')">i</span>

### Level 4: Excellence (99th percentile capability)

**Service**: Leadership role in community organisations, coordinating volunteer efforts and driving measurable improvements in community outcomes <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-service')">i</span>

**Civic Engagement**: Significant influence on local policy and community direction through sustained, informed participation in civic institutions <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-civic')">i</span>

**Social Capital**: A central node in your community's social fabric, trusted across diverse groups and able to mobilise collective action when needed <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-social')">i</span>

### Level 5: Mastery (99.9th percentile capability)

**Service**: Transformative community impact through years of dedicated service -- you have measurably improved the quality of life in your community through sustained, strategic contribution <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-service')">i</span>

**Civic Engagement**: Deep, sustained civic leadership that has shaped your community's trajectory -- you are recognised as an essential voice in local governance and community development <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-civic')">i</span>

**Social Capital**: Your community is measurably more connected, trusting, and resilient because of the social infrastructure you have helped build over years of deliberate investment <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-social')">i</span>
