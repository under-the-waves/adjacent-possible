# Transportation

## Why Transportation Matters

How you move through the world shapes your daily experience more than most people realise. Commute times range from 40 to 80 minutes on average, with public transport users often spending considerably longer than active travellers <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-commute-times')">i</span>. People who walk or cycle to work report the highest levels of commute satisfaction, while public transport users report the lowest. Active commuters experience greater enjoyment and feelings of physical and mental health compared to those who commute by car or public transit <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-active-transport')">i</span>.

The wellbeing impact extends beyond the commute itself. Walking to work is associated with increased leisure time satisfaction and reduced strain. Switching from car travel to active travel shows significant positive effects on overall psychological wellbeing <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-wellbeing')">i</span>. Transportation choices also have financial implications -- car ownership typically costs thousands per year in depreciation, insurance, fuel, and maintenance -- and environmental consequences that increasingly factor into personal values. Optimising your transportation is one of the highest-leverage daily life improvements available.

## Transportation Values

Your approach to transportation depends on what aspects you value most. This guide balances three core values, with percentages indicating the relative weight given to each in our recommendations.

### Reliability (40%)
Consistent, dependable transportation that gets you where you need to be on time without stress or uncertainty. This includes vehicle maintenance, route planning, backup options, and minimising the risk of breakdowns, delays, or cancellations. People who prioritise this value invest in dependable systems that remove transportation anxiety.

### Efficiency (35%)
Minimising the time, money, and cognitive effort spent on transportation. This includes optimising commute routes, reducing unnecessary trips, combining errands, and choosing modes that maximise productive or enjoyable use of travel time. Those who prioritise this value treat transportation as a resource to optimise rather than an unavoidable cost.

### Sustainability (25%)
Choosing transportation options that minimise environmental impact and support long-term health. This includes active transport (walking, cycling), public transit, electric vehicles, and designing your life to reduce transportation needs. People who prioritise this value align their movement choices with their environmental and health values.

## Benchmarks by Level

Transportation satisfaction varies dramatically by mode and duration. Active commuters (walkers and cyclists) consistently report the highest satisfaction, while long car commutes and public transit generate the lowest. Average commute times of 40 -- 80 minutes represent a significant daily time investment, and most people accept their transportation patterns as fixed rather than optimisable. Deliberate transportation design -- choosing where to live, how to commute, and how to manage mobility based on evidence -- is genuinely uncommon.

### Level 1: Awareness

**Reliability**: Know your current transportation costs, typical journey times, and failure points (delays, breakdowns, missed connections) <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-reliability')">i</span>

**Efficiency**: Track your total weekly transportation time and cost, including commute, errands, and leisure travel <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-efficiency')">i</span>

**Sustainability**: Understand the environmental and health impact of your current transportation patterns <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-sustainability')">i</span>

### Level 2: Foundation (80th percentile capability)

**Reliability**: Dependable daily transportation with a backup plan, well-maintained vehicle or reliable transit access, and rare unplanned disruptions <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-reliability')">i</span>

**Efficiency**: Total commute under 30 minutes each way, errands batched and route-optimised, and transportation costs below 15% of income <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-efficiency')">i</span>

**Sustainability**: At least 2 -- 3 trips per week made by active transport or public transit instead of car <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-sustainability')">i</span>

### Level 3: Proficiency (95th percentile capability)

**Reliability**: Near-zero transportation disruptions through proactive maintenance, multiple backup options, and route flexibility <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-reliability')">i</span>

**Efficiency**: Commute under 20 minutes or productively used, transportation costs below 10% of income, and minimal time lost to unnecessary travel <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-efficiency')">i</span>

**Sustainability**: Primary commute by active transport or public transit, with car use limited to situations where alternatives are impractical <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-sustainability')">i</span>

### Level 4: Excellence (99th percentile capability)

**Reliability**: Transportation is never a source of stress -- all modes are dependable, well-maintained, and seamlessly integrated into daily life <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-reliability')">i</span>

**Efficiency**: Commute is either negligible (remote work, walkable) or genuinely enjoyable, with transportation costs minimal relative to income <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-efficiency')">i</span>

**Sustainability**: Majority of transportation by active or zero-emission modes, with life deliberately structured to minimise transportation needs <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-sustainability')">i</span>

### Level 5: Mastery (99.9th percentile capability)

**Reliability**: Complete transportation independence -- multiple reliable options available at any time, with the resources and flexibility to adapt instantly to any disruption <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-reliability')">i</span>

**Efficiency**: Transportation optimised to near-zero wasted time and minimal cost, with location and lifestyle choices that eliminate unnecessary travel entirely <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-efficiency')">i</span>

**Sustainability**: Fully sustainable transportation with net-zero or positive environmental impact, active transport as the default mode, and health benefits that compound over years <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-sustainability')">i</span>
