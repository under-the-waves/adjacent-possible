# Transportation

## What is Transportation?

How you get from place to place in daily life -- your commute, errands, travel, and the systems, vehicles, and habits that support your mobility.

## Why Transportation matters

- **It consumes significant time** -- Average commute times range from 40 to 80 minutes, and most people accept their transportation patterns as fixed rather than optimisable <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-commute-times')">i</span>.
- **Mode choice affects wellbeing** -- Active commuters (walkers and cyclists) report the highest satisfaction, while long car and public transit commutes generate the lowest. Switching to active travel shows significant positive effects on psychological wellbeing <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-active-transport')">i</span> <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('research-wellbeing')">i</span>.
- **Financial weight** -- Car ownership typically costs thousands per year in depreciation, insurance, fuel, and maintenance. Transportation is one of the largest household expenses after housing.
- **High leverage for improvement** -- Unlike many life areas, transportation can often be substantially improved through a single decision (moving closer to work, changing commute mode, or restructuring errand patterns).

## Transportation Values

Your approach to transportation depends on what aspects you value most. This guide balances three core values, with percentages indicating the relative weight given to each in our recommendations.

### Efficiency (40%)
Minimising the time, money, and cognitive effort spent on transportation. This includes optimising commute routes, reducing unnecessary trips, combining errands, and choosing modes that maximise productive or enjoyable use of travel time. People who prioritise this value treat transportation as a resource to optimise.

### Comfort (35%)
The physical and psychological experience of your daily transportation -- pleasant, stress-free, and compatible with your preferences. This includes vehicle quality, commute environment, protection from weather, and the overall pleasantness of your travel experience. People who prioritise this value invest in making the journey itself enjoyable.

### Safety (25%)
Minimising the risk of injury, accident, or incident during transportation. This includes vehicle safety features, route selection, defensive driving or cycling practices, and choosing modes with strong safety records. People who prioritise this value make travel decisions based on risk reduction.

## Benchmarks by Level

Transportation satisfaction varies dramatically by mode and duration. Active commuters (walkers and cyclists) consistently report the highest satisfaction, while long car commutes and public transit generate the lowest. Average commute times of 40 -- 80 minutes represent a significant daily time investment, and most people accept their transportation patterns as fixed rather than optimisable. Deliberate transportation design -- choosing where to live, how to commute, and how to manage mobility based on evidence -- is genuinely uncommon.

### Level 1: Awareness

**Efficiency**: Track your total weekly transportation time and cost, including commute, errands, and leisure travel <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-efficiency')">i</span>

**Comfort**: Know which aspects of your daily transportation are pleasant and which cause stress or discomfort <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-comfort')">i</span>

**Safety**: Understand the risks associated with your current transportation modes and routes <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level1-safety')">i</span>

### Level 2: Foundation (80th percentile capability)

**Efficiency**: Total commute under 30 minutes each way, errands batched and route-optimised, and transportation costs below 15% of income <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-efficiency')">i</span>

**Comfort**: Daily transportation is reliably comfortable -- a well-maintained vehicle or pleasant transit experience with protection from weather and minimal stress <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-comfort')">i</span>

**Safety**: Vehicle or mode in good safety condition, safe route choices, and basic defensive practices that reduce accident risk <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level2-safety')">i</span>

### Level 3: Proficiency (95th percentile capability)

**Efficiency**: Commute under 20 minutes or productively used, transportation costs below 10% of income, and minimal time lost to unnecessary travel <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-efficiency')">i</span>

**Comfort**: Transportation that is genuinely enjoyable -- a pleasant commute environment, reliable climate control, and a travel experience you look forward to rather than endure <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-comfort')">i</span>

**Safety**: Proactive safety measures -- advanced vehicle safety features, well-practised defensive habits, and route selection that minimises exposure to risk <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level3-safety')">i</span>

### Level 4: Excellence (99th percentile capability)

**Efficiency**: Commute is either negligible (remote work, walkable) or genuinely enjoyable, with transportation costs minimal relative to income <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-efficiency')">i</span>

**Comfort**: Transportation is a consistently pleasant experience -- premium vehicle or ideal commute conditions, with no aspect of daily travel causing discomfort or frustration <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-comfort')">i</span>

**Safety**: Near-zero risk exposure through optimal vehicle safety, defensive expertise, and life structured to minimise time in high-risk transportation contexts <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level4-safety')">i</span>

### Level 5: Mastery (99.9th percentile capability)

**Efficiency**: Transportation optimised to near-zero wasted time and minimal cost, with location and lifestyle choices that eliminate unnecessary travel entirely <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-efficiency')">i</span>

**Comfort**: Every aspect of transportation is a pleasure -- whether driving, cycling, walking, or flying, your travel experience is consistently among the best available and a genuine source of enjoyment <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-comfort')">i</span>

**Safety**: Complete transportation safety mastery -- negligible accident risk through a combination of vehicle choice, skill, route design, and lifestyle choices that minimise exposure to danger <span class="info-icon" onclick="showReasoning('level5-safety')">i</span>
